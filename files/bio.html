RADIOHEAD

Radiohead is an English rock band formed in Abingdon, Oxfordshire, in 1985. The band consists of Thom Yorke (vocals, guitar, piano), brothers Jonny Greenwood (lead guitar, keyboards) and Colin Greenwood (bass), Ed O'Brien (guitar, backing vocals), and Philip Selway (drums, percussion). They have worked with producer Nigel Godrich and cover artist Stanley Donwood since 1994.

MEET THE BAND

THOM YORKE

Lead Vocals, Guitar, Piano

Thomas Edward Yorke is the lead vocalist and principal songwriter of Radiohead. Known for his falsetto voice and abstract lyrics, Yorke is regarded as one of the most influential musicians of his generation. Beyond Radiohead, he has released solo albums and collaborated with various artists.

JONNY GREENWOOD

Lead Guitar, Keyboards, Ondes Martenot

Jonathan Richard Guy Greenwood is a multi-instrumentalist and the lead guitarist of Radiohead. He's known for his innovative guitar techniques and has also composed several acclaimed film scores, including "There Will Be Blood" and "Phantom Thread."

COLIN GREENWOOD

Bass Guitar

Colin Charles Greenwood is the bassist for Radiohead. His melodic bass lines are a key component of the band's sound. Colin is also known for his photography work and has documented much of the band's history through his lens.

ED O'BRIEN

Guitar, Backing Vocals

Edward John O'Brien is a guitarist and backing vocalist for Radiohead. He's known for his use of effects pedals and ambient soundscapes. In 2020, he released his debut solo album "Earth" under the name EOB.

PHILIP SELWAY

Drums, Percussion

Philip James Selway is the drummer for Radiohead. His precise and dynamic drumming style has been crucial to the band's sound. He has also released solo albums showcasing his songwriting and vocal abilities.
